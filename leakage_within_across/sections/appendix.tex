\section{Variable definitions}\label{app:variables}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{firm cost share} (\eqref{eq:fcs}): pre-shock firm-level carbon cost share, time-invariant. Numerator: 2013--2016 average annual allowance shortfall in tCO$_2$ priced at the year-specific EUA. Denominator: 2010--2012 average total operating cost from Annual Accounts. Falls back to the earliest available three-year window for firms without 2010--2012 data.
    \item \textbf{nace exposure} (\eqref{eq:nace_exposure}): sales-weighted average firm cost share within a NACE 4-digit category, time-invariant; equals zero for categories with no ETS sellers.
    \item \textbf{share top}: annual share of the most-exposed ETS supplier $j^*(b,n)$ in cell $(b,n)$'s total spending in that NACE 4-digit category.
    \item \textbf{pair exposure}: $\text{firm cost share}_{j^*(b,n)} \times \bar{s}_{j^*(b,n)}$, where $\bar{s}_{j^*(b,n)} = \overline{\text{spending from } b \text{ to } j^*}_{2010..2014} / \overline{\text{total inputs of } b}_{2010..2014}$. Units: percentage of buyer's total input bill at risk if $j^*$'s carbon shock fully passes through and the buyer does not substitute.
    \item \textbf{Post}: $\mathbf{1}[t \geq 2015]$.
    \item \textbf{regulated}: $\mathbf{1}[\text{nace exposure}_n > 0]$.
\end{itemize}

\section{Pass-through: CMdG-style regulated-vs-unregulated event study}\label{app:passthrough_cmdj}

We complement the headline panel-LP elasticity in \S\ref{sec:passthrough} with an event-study replication of \citet[eq.~1]{CosterMejeanDiGiovanni2024} on Belgian monthly NACE 4-digit PPI data. The specification is exactly CMdG's:
\begin{equation}
    \log \text{PPI}_{s,t} \;=\; \sum_{\tau \neq 2004} \beta_\tau \,\mathbf{1}[s \in \text{regulated}]\,\mathbf{1}[\text{year}(t) = \tau] \;+\; \alpha_{s, \,\text{month}(t)} \;+\; \delta_{\text{year}(t)} \;+\; \varepsilon_{s,t}
    \label{eq:passthrough_cmdj}
\end{equation}
estimated on the 2000m1--2022m12 monthly panel with sector $\times$ calendar-month fixed effects $\alpha_{s, \text{month}(t)}$ (controlling for sector-specific seasonality, as in CMdG) and year fixed effects $\delta_{\text{year}(t)}$. Standard errors are clustered on NACE 4-digit. ``Regulated'' is CMdG's tight definition (seven NACE 2-digit codes \{17, 19, 20, 23, 24, 25, 35\}, per their Table~A.5 col~(1)/(4)). The reference year is 2004, the last pre-ETS year.

This specification differs from the headline panel-LP in \S\ref{sec:passthrough} in two ways. First, identification is binary (regulated vs unregulated NACE 2-digit) rather than continuous in $\omega$. Second, no carbon-policy shock is used: the variation comes from the relative time-series of regulated vs unregulated PPI, not from regulatory news events. The CMdG specification is thus less structural — it documents that prices in regulated sectors rose faster than in unregulated sectors over Phase~II and beyond, but it does not isolate the EU ETS as the cause. We report it because it is the standard reference in the literature \citep{CosterMejeanDiGiovanni2024}, and because it provides a sanity check on the panel-LP result at a different identifying margin.

\paragraph{Result.} The year-by-year coefficients show a clear structural break around 2005 (Phase~I start), with regulated PPI rising 2--4 log-points relative to unregulated by 2005--2007, then 7--10 log-points through Phase~II, and 9--14 log-points by Phase~IV. Phase-aggregated coefficients (relative to the 2000--2004 pre-ETS reference period):
\[
    \widehat{\beta}_{\text{Phase 1}} = +0.088^{***}, \quad
    \widehat{\beta}_{\text{Phase 2}} = +0.138^{***}, \quad
    \widehat{\beta}_{\text{Phase 3}} = +0.098^{**}, \quad
    \widehat{\beta}_{\text{Phase 4}} = +0.193^{***}.
\]
A pre-trend is visible: pre-ETS coefficients drift from $-0.077$ in 2000 toward zero at the 2004 reference, suggesting the regulated-vs-unregulated PPI gap was already closing before the EU~ETS began. Linear extrapolation of the pre-trend forward predicts $\widehat{\beta}_h \approx +0.08$ at $h = 2008$ from the trend alone; the actual Phase~II average ($+0.138$) is meaningfully above that extrapolation, but the pre-trend leaves the design less than a pristine parallel-trends test. The CMdG specification on Belgian data is therefore consistent with positive and economically large pass-through, but with a parallel-trends caveat that motivates the headline reliance on the Käenzig-shock identification of \S\ref{sec:passthrough} as the cleaner identifying source.

\section{Pass-through: aggregate-PPI sample-window robustness}\label{app:passthrough_aggregate_robust}

We re-estimate the aggregate local-projection of equation~(\ref{eq:passthrough_aggregate}) on two alternative sample windows: the full Käenzig window 2005m1--2019m12 (including Phase~I), and the Phase-III-only window 2013m1--2019m12. Both produce IRFs of the same hump shape and similar peak magnitude as the headline 2008m1--2019m12 estimate, with wider confidence bands reflecting the smaller (Phase III only) or noisier (Phase~I included) samples. In both robustness samples the point estimate is positive at the majority of horizons (32 of 37 in 2005--2019, 31 of 37 in 2013--2019), with several horizons significant at 68\% and none significant at 68\% in the negative direction except at the long-horizon tail (consistent with mean-reversion of a temporary shock).

\section{Pass-through: sectoral $\widehat{\gamma}_h$ on Phase~III only}\label{app:passthrough_gamma_phase3}

Figure~\ref{fig:passthrough_gamma_phase3} re-estimates the sectoral panel-LP of equation~(\ref{eq:passthrough_panel}) on 2013m1--2019m12 (Phase~III only). The IRF is positive at 25 of 37 horizons with a peak of $\widehat{\gamma}_h \approx 1.76$ at $h = 22$ — closely tracking the headline 2008m1--2019m12 IRF in shape and peak location — but with materially wider confidence bands. A direct comparison of the two windows' point estimates yields $|z| < 1.96$ at 36 of 37 horizons (mean $|z| = 0.54$), so the two windows are estimating statistically the same $\gamma_h$. The shorter sample reduces identifying variation (479 vs 821 sector-year cells with $\omega > 0$, 84 vs 144 months) and thereby inflates standard errors by approximately a factor of 1.7 — enough that the negative point estimates at some horizons fall well within sampling noise around the headline path.
