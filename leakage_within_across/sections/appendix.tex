\section{Variable definitions}\label{app:variables}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{firm cost share} (\eqref{eq:fcs}): pre-shock firm-level carbon cost share, time-invariant. Numerator: 2013--2016 average annual allowance shortfall in tCO$_2$ priced at the year-specific EUA. Denominator: 2010--2012 average total operating cost from Annual Accounts. Falls back to the earliest available three-year window for firms without 2010--2012 data.
    \item \textbf{nace exposure} (\eqref{eq:nace_exposure}): sales-weighted average firm cost share within a NACE 4-digit category, time-invariant; equals zero for categories with no ETS sellers.
    \item \textbf{share top}: annual share of the most-exposed ETS supplier $j^*(b,n)$ in cell $(b,n)$'s total spending in that NACE 4-digit category.
    \item \textbf{pair exposure}: $\text{firm cost share}_{j^*(b,n)} \times \bar{s}_{j^*(b,n)}$, where $\bar{s}_{j^*(b,n)} = \overline{\text{spending from } b \text{ to } j^*}_{2010..2014} / \overline{\text{total inputs of } b}_{2010..2014}$. Units: percentage of buyer's total input bill at risk if $j^*$'s carbon shock fully passes through and the buyer does not substitute.
    \item \textbf{Post}: $\mathbf{1}[t \geq 2015]$.
    \item \textbf{regulated}: $\mathbf{1}[\text{nace exposure}_n > 0]$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{EUA price response: shock vs surprise}\label{app:passthrough_rh}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

We compare the dynamic regression of the cumulative log-EUA price change on the Känzig Shock with the same regression run on the Känzig Surprise. The exercise is the time-series first stage that sits behind the LP-IV in §\ref{app:passthrough_iv}; we report it on its own to make the relative tightness of the two instruments visible.

For each instrument $z \in \{\text{Shock}_m, \text{Surprise}_m\}$ and horizon $h$ we run
\begin{equation}
    \log \text{EUA}_{m+h} - \log \text{EUA}_{m-1} \;=\; R_h \cdot z_m + \sum_{j=1}^{6} \phi_j \log \text{EUA}_{m-j} + u_{m, h},
\end{equation}
on the 2005m1--2019m12 sample with heteroskedasticity-robust standard errors.

\subsection{Sectoral PPI on (CPShock $\times$ rolling $\omega^{sh}$)}\label{app:passthrough_rolling}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

Specification (C) in the main text fixes the exposure measure $\omega^{sh}_s$ on 2015--2016. As a robustness check we let exposure vary year by year, using \emph{previous-year} shortage-over-cost as the predetermined sector weight, and run the panel local projection
\begin{equation}
    \log \text{PPI}_{s, m+h} - \log \text{PPI}_{s, m-1} \;=\; \gamma_h \cdot \big( \text{CPShock}_m \cdot \omega^{sh}_{s, \text{year}(m) - 1} \big) + \alpha_s + \delta_m + \varepsilon_{s, m, h}
\end{equation}
on the 2006m1--2019m12 monthly NACE 4-digit panel, with sector and year-month fixed effects and standard errors clustered at the NACE 4-digit level.

\subsection{Rambachan--Roth sensitivity for column 4}\label{app:passthrough_rr}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

The headline event-study figure \ref{fig:passthrough_eventstudy} reports $\hat\beta_\tau$ for $X_s = \omega^{sh}_s$ year by year, with 2016 as the omitted reference. The differential turns on at 2017 and rises through 2022. We test how robust the first post-treatment coefficient $\hat\beta_{2017}$ is to a relaxation of the parallel-trends assumption, following \citet{rambachanroth2023}.

We report two sets of bounds. The relative-magnitudes restriction $\Delta^{RM}(\bar M)$ asks: if the post-treatment violation of parallel trends is at most $\bar M$ times the largest pre-treatment violation, what is the bias-robust confidence set for $\hat\beta_{2017}$? The smoothness restriction $\Delta^{SD}(M)$ asks: if the second differences of the post-treatment trend are bounded by $M$, what is the FLCI?

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{phase4_ppi_eventstudy_omega_sh_honestdid.pdf}
    \caption{Rambachan-Roth (2023) relative-magnitudes sensitivity bounds for $\hat\beta_{2017}$ from the event-study form of column 4 of Table~\ref{tab:passthrough_did} (Figure~\ref{fig:passthrough_eventstudy}). The leftmost interval is the original 95\% confidence interval; subsequent intervals impose increasingly large bounds $\bar M$ on post-treatment parallel-trends violations relative to the maximum pre-treatment violation. Source: \texttt{phase4\_ppi\_did\_2017\_eventstudy.R}; tabular RM and SD bounds in \texttt{phase4\_ppi\_eventstudy\_omega\_sh\_RM.csv} and \texttt{\_SD.csv}.}
    \label{fig:app_honestdid}
\end{figure}

The original interval excludes zero. Under a relative-magnitudes restriction with $\bar M = 0.5$ (post-treatment violation up to half of the worst pre-treatment violation), the coefficient is no longer statistically distinguishable from zero. We report the result without claiming robustness to large violations of parallel trends; the RM bounds quantify exactly how strong the parallel-trends assumption needs to be for the column-4 result to survive.
