A precondition for either margin of leakage is that carbon costs reach buyers as a relative-price increase on regulated suppliers. We document this precondition on the Belgian panel via a panel local-projection on the \citet{kanzig2023unequal} structural carbon-policy shock, recovering a horizon-by-horizon pass-through elasticity. A regulated-vs-unregulated event-study replication of \citet{CosterMejeanDiGiovanni2024} on Belgian monthly PPI is reported as a robustness exhibit in Appendix~\ref{app:passthrough_cmdj}.

\paragraph{Specification.} Following the panel local-projection specification of \citet[eq.~10]{kanzig2023unequal}, on the 2010m1--2019m12 monthly NACE 4-digit PPI panel we estimate
\begin{equation}
    \log \text{PPI}_{s,m+h} - \log \text{PPI}_{s,m-1} \;=\; \gamma_h \cdot \big(\omega_{s, t(m)} \times \text{CPShock}_m\big) \;+\; \sum_{j=1}^{6} \theta_{h,j} \log \text{PPI}_{s, m-j} \;+\; \alpha_s \;+\; \delta_m \;+\; \varepsilon_{s,m,h}
    \label{eq:passthrough_strategy1}
\end{equation}
separately at each horizon $h \in \{0, 1, \ldots, 36\}$ months. $\text{CPShock}_m$ is the \citet{kanzig2023unequal} VAR-identified carbon-policy structural shock; $\omega_{s, t(m)}$ is sector $s$'s pre-shock cost-share exposure (defined below); $\sum_j \theta_{h,j} \log \text{PPI}_{s, m-j}$ is the lag-augmentation that controls for LHS persistence; $\alpha_s$ and $\delta_m$ are sector and year-month fixed effects; standard errors are clustered on NACE 4-digit. The sample window matches \citet{kanzig2023unequal}'s baseline VAR (1999m1--2019m12), trimmed at 2010m1 by ICAP daily EUA coverage.

The treatment intensity $\omega_{s, t(m)}$ is the sector's pre-shock marginal-cost-share exposure to ETS allowance costs:
\begin{equation}
    \omega_{s, t(m)} \;=\; \frac{\sum_{j \in \mathcal{E}_s} \text{emissions}_{j, t(m)-1} \cdot \text{EUA}_{t(m)-1}}{\sum_{j \in \mathcal{E}_s} \text{total cost}_{j, t(m)-1}},
    \label{eq:omega}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{E}_s$ is the set of ETS firms in sector $s$ and $t(m)$ is the calendar year of month $m$. The numerator uses gross emissions (not unallocated shortage) following the windfall-profit logic of \citet{fabra2014}: under marginal pricing, the relevant cost-share is the opportunity cost of emissions, which is gross emissions $\times$ EUA price regardless of free allocation. $\omega_{s, t(m)}$ updates annually so that the cross-sectional ranking of sectors by ETS exposure tracks evolving emission intensities. $\omega_{s, t(m)}$ is set to zero for non-ETS NACE 4-digit sectors, which then contribute to the fixed-effect estimation but not to identification of $\gamma_h$.

\paragraph{Pass-through elasticity.} The panel-LP coefficient $\widehat{\gamma}_h$ measures the cumulative log-PPI response over $[m-1, m+h]$ to a unit Käenzig structural shock at month $m$, scaled by sector cost-share exposure. We convert it to a pass-through elasticity by dividing by the aggregate cumulative EUA-price response to the same unit shock:
\begin{equation}
    \widehat{\beta}_h \;=\; \frac{\widehat{\gamma}_h}{\widehat{R}}, \qquad
    \widehat{R} \;=\; \frac{1}{13}\sum_{h = 12}^{24} \widehat{R}_h, \qquad
    \widehat{R}_h \;\text{from} \quad \log \text{EUA}_{m+h} - \log \text{EUA}_{m-1} \;=\; R_h \cdot \text{CPShock}_m + \text{lags},
    \label{eq:passthrough_beta}
\end{equation}
where $\widehat{R}$ is the average cumulative EUA-price response over the mid-horizon window $h \in [12, 24]$ in which the EUA IRF is well-identified.\footnote{Using horizon-specific $\widehat{R}_h$ produces unstable $\widehat{\beta}_h$: at the monthly frequency CPShock explains less than 1\% of $\Delta \log p^{\text{EUA}}$ (the LP-IV power problem flagged by \citet[App.~C.5]{kanzig2023unequal}), so when $\widehat{R}_h$ is near zero the ratio blows up. Averaging over $[12, 24]$ gives a stable denominator while preserving horizon-$h$ variation in $\widehat{\gamma}_h$.} The interpretation is direct: for a sector with cost-share exposure $\omega$, a 1\% increase in EUA prices passes through to sector PPI as $\widehat{\beta}_h \cdot \omega$\,\% at horizon $h$. $\widehat{\beta}_h = 1$ corresponds to full Shephard's-lemma pass-through.

\paragraph{Result.} Figure~\ref{fig:passthrough_iv} reports $\widehat{\beta}_h$. Pass-through is incomplete on impact ($\widehat{\beta}_0 \approx 0.16$), builds gradually over the first year, and stabilises near full pass-through at horizons of one year and beyond ($\widehat{\beta}_h \in [0.7, 1.5]$ for $h \geq 9$, with point estimates clustered around 1). The estimated $\widehat{R} \approx 0.092$ over the mid-horizon window, meaning a unit Käenzig shock produces a 9.2\% cumulative log-EUA increase 12--24 months out — a magnitude consistent with the EUA-price dynamics in \citet{kanzig2023unequal}'s SVAR Figure~2. The 95\% confidence band is wide at every horizon — a known limitation of LP-IV identification with high-frequency policy surprises against macroeconomic outcomes, discussed in detail by \citet[App.~C.5]{kanzig2023unequal} — but the band excludes zero or sits just above zero across most of the IRF, and is consistent with full pass-through ($\widehat{\beta}_h = 1$) at every horizon $h \geq 6$.
