% Argument arc:
%   1. Carbon pricing's first-order effect on regulated firms is to raise their
%      marginal cost. Whether the policy *reduces emissions* depends on what
%      happens to those costs once they leave the firm: passed forward to
%      buyers (pass-through), absorbed in margins, or avoided by buyers
%      switching to unregulated sources (leakage).
%   2. Existing micro-evidence on pass-through is sector- or product-specific
%      and rarely uses panel-LP / SVAR identification of the carbon-policy
%      shock; existing leakage evidence (CdGM 2024 for France) finds large
%      cross-border substitution but cannot speak to *domestic* leakage
%      because France lacks firm-to-firm transaction data.
%   3. Belgium is uniquely instrumented: full EUTL × Annual Accounts × B2B
%      transaction × Customs panel, all linked at the VAT level. We use this
%      to estimate pass-through and both leakage margins on the same economy.
%   4. Headline: positive pass-through (Phase 2 firmly, Phase 4 partially);
%      no detectable cross-border leakage; no detectable B2B leakage under
%      the continuous treatment. ETS costs were paid forward in Belgium, but
%      buyers did not substitute away.
%
